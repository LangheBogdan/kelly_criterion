The Formula

  • f*: The fraction of your bankroll to wager.
  • b: The decimal odds received on the wager, minus 1.
  • p: The probability of winning.
  • q: The probability of losing (which is 1 - p).

What is the Kelly Criterion?

A mathematical formula for bet sizing that aims to maximize the long-term growth rate of a bankroll.

Practical Considerations

  • Fractional Kelly: Most practitioners use a fraction to reduce risk.
  • Estimation Errors: The formula is very sensitive to the 'p' input.
  • Discipline: This is a bankroll management strategy, not a system for picking winners.
